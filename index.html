02 — Meanwhile, in the water you didn't touch

The DIY Chronicles

What grows
while you wait

Turn your back for one warm weekend, and the water begins to change. In the still, unattended deep end, an entire ecosystem stakes its claim.

First bloom the ALGAE — green algae, mustard algae, and black algae.

Then spread the MOLDS and BACTERIA in the dark — white water mold unfurling like wet tissue through the skimmer, and Pseudomonas, E. coli close behind.

Deeper still wait the PARASITES — Cryptosporidium, Giardia, and Naegleria fowleri, the brain-eating amoeba.

This is the water you inherit the moment you look away.

Every one of them loses to balanced water.
