The basics

Six things to understand.

01
Why is my pool green?

Algae. It blooms the moment chlorine drops and the water warms — one hot weekend with a low sanitizer is all it takes. The fix is almost never draining: shock hard, run the filter, and brush.

02
Cloudy, but not green?

That is chemistry, not algae. Usually high pH, or particles too fine for the filter to grab. Balance the water, add a clarifier, and let the pump turn it over.

03
How often should I test?

Twice a week in summer, weekly the rest of the year. Heat, rain, and swimmers all move the numbers fast. (On a plan, we test every visit and log it.)

04
Is it safe to swim after a storm?

Test first. Runoff dilutes chlorine and dumps in contaminants and phosphates — prime algae food. Rebalance before anyone gets in.

05
Salt vs. chlorine?

A salt pool still sanitizes with chlorine — it just generates its own from salt instead of you adding it. Softer feel, less handling, same chemistry underneath.

06
How long should the pump run?

Long enough to turn the whole pool over once a day — about 8 to 12 hours in summer heat. Circulation is what keeps chlorine working and algae from settling.
