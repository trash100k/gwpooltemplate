Meanwhile, in the water you didn't touch

The DIY Chronicles

What grows
while you wait

Turn your back for one warm weekend, and the water begins to change. In the still, unattended deep end, an entire ecosystem stakes its claim.

First bloom the ALGAE — green algae, mustard algae, black algae, blue-green cyanobacteria, and the creeping pink slime that stains the tile line.

Then spread the MOLDS in the dark — white water mold, unfurling like wet tissue through the skimmer and the returns.

In their wake creep the BACTERIA — Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Legionella pneumophila, Escherichia coli, Shigella, and Campylobacter.

Deeper still wait the PARASITES — Cryptosporidium, Giardia, Acanthamoeba, and Naegleria fowleri, the brain-eating amoeba.

And drifting on every warm current, the VIRUSES — norovirus, adenovirus, hepatitis A, echovirus, and coxsackievirus.

This is the water you inherit the moment you look away.
